What ACE Builds

A civilization. Not a program.

Every system a community needs to govern itself, sustain itself, and pass itself forward to the next generation.

🏠
Housing

Lease-to-own on tribal sovereign land. The ACE Community Provident Fund builds equity with every paycheck. Zero Homelessness Mandate written into the governing charter.

🏫
Education

K–12 ACE Freedom Schools through ACE University. Bureau of Indian Education approved. Pan-African and Indigenous studies at every grade. Tuition-free for ACE residents.

🏥
Healthcare

Community clinic to full hospital. ACE MediSave — no medical debt. Modern medicine co-equal with Indigenous and African traditional healing. Black maternal mortality eliminated by design.

💰
Economy

ACE owns the infrastructure layer. Free enterprise above it. Majority Lending Institution network. Community Investment Bonds from $500. Every dollar circulates within the community before leaving.

🏛
Governance

Three-branch government: Community Assembly, Executive Council, Community Court. Co-equal tribal co-governance. The ACE Governing Charter as supreme law. No outside authority governs ACE.

🛡
Public Safety

Tiered peacekeeping — the right responder for every call. 60% of calls handled by unarmed community care workers. Restorative justice replaces incarceration as the default.

🌿
Food & Energy

51% tribal-majority agricultural cooperative. 80% halal food establishments. Full energy independence. Food grown on ACE land, by ACE farmers, for ACE families.

📺
Media

ACE Media Network: TV, podcast, print, streaming. The story of Black and Native America — told by Black and Native America. Community-owned. No external editorial control.

The ACE Social Contract

Four guarantees. Every resident. By retirement.

Not as aspiration. As architecture. Written into the ACE Governing Charter and enforced by the community’s own constitutional institutions.

1

Home Ownership

A home owned free and clear within ACE Territory — built through the ACE Community Provident Fund Housing Account with every paycheck.

2

Retirement Dignity

A retirement income sufficient to live with dignity within the ACE economy — built through mandatory community savings with employer matching.

3

Healthcare Without Debt

Comprehensive healthcare for themselves and their dependents — pre-funded through ACE MediSave. No bill. No debt. No fear.

4

Education for Their Children

Free education from early childhood through university — within the ACE school system, grounded in the culture and history of both peoples.

This is the promise ACE makes. Not as aspiration. As architecture.

The Four Phases

From movement to civilization — in 25 years.

A sequenced, milestone-based development plan built on existing federal law and tribal sovereignty doctrine.

Phase 1 Years 0–2
The Movement

Form ACE’s legal entities. Recruit the Native American Co-Chair — the single most urgent pre-institutional act. Forge tribal partnerships. Build national grassroots awareness. Secure founding capital from the Black and Native American community of means.

Phase 2 Years 2–7
Territory

Execute the first Tribal Partnership Agreement — 150,000 acres released in milestone increments. Break ground. Build water, energy, roads, and first homes. Launch the community health clinic, ACE Freedom School, ACE Credit Union, and agricultural cooperative.

Phase 3 Years 7–15
The City

Scale to 300,000 pioneer residents. All municipal systems operational — three-branch governance, community courts, ACE hospital, ACE University, media network, and a diversified economy producing energy, food, technology, and culture for export.

Phase 4 Years 15–25
Civilization

Replicate nationally. 10+ ACE cities across five formally defined regions. ACE University system. International trade with Africa and the Caribbean. A $50 billion annual economy. 32 million people served by institutions they own, govern, and pass to their children.
